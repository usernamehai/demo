Khabib Nurmagomedov

Retired UFC Lightweight Champion

Khabib Abdulmanapovich Nurmagomedov, born on September 20, 1988, in the mountainous village of Sildi in the Dagestan region of Russia, rose to prominence as one of the greatest mixed martial artists (MMA) of all time. A devout Muslim and a family man, Khabib’s life story is deeply intertwined with his cultural heritage, family values, and an unwavering spirit that drove him to unparalleled success in the UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship). Khabib’s story began in a traditional Dagestani family. His father, Abdulmanap Nurmagomedov, was a highly respected wrestler and martial arts coach who played an instrumental role in shaping Khabib’s career. Growing up, young Khabib was introduced to combat sports at an early age. Under the tutelage of his father, he began wrestling at just eight years old. His daily routines were rigorous and involved various martial arts disciplines, helping mold him into an exceptional athlete with an indomitable will to succeed.

  • UFC Lightweight Champion (2018–2020)
  • Undefeated professional MMA record: 29-0
  • Known for wrestling and grappling skills
